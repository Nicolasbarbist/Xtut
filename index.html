Judaism

Introduction

Judaism is one of the oldest monotheistic religions, originating in the Hebrew Bible (also known as the Tanakh) and the teachings of the Talmud. It encompasses a rich tradition and culture.

Beliefs

Jews believe in the one God who revealed himself to Abraham, Moses, and the Hebrew prophets. They also believe in the importance of following God's commandments, as outlined in the Torah.

Practices

Key practices in Judaism include prayer, study of religious texts, observance of dietary laws (kashrut), and participation in rituals such as circumcision, bar/bat mitzvah, and marriage.

Holidays

Major Jewish holidays include Rosh Hashanah, Yom Kippur, Passover, Hanukkah, and Sukkot. These holidays often involve special prayers, rituals, and festive meals.
